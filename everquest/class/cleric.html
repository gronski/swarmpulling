An Introduction to Cleric Swarming

Learn the techniques needed to master cleric swarm pulling

Clerics are one of the most powerful classes for swarm pulling in Everquest. One of the main reasons behind this, is due to the ability to cast the Divine spell line and the ability to use the Divine Hammer of Consternation which is a must-have for any cleric! This weapon allows you to proc the Divine Aura spell in the middle of combat, with no recast times or lock outs. Clerics, with their heavy armor and arsenal of utility spells, are perfectly suited to begin mass pulling at lower levels.

At level 50 clerics are able to begin using the ward spell line and at level 71 the Vow spell line. These spells are the backbone of the cleric’s ability to swarm pull. The vow spell line will allow the cleric to proc a heal over time spell on themselves while also procing a substantial direct damage spell against their target. The ward spell applies a damage shield buff to your character that has a chance to strike back against any attackers. This isn’t your normal damage shield, as it packs a punch and it also works through the divine spells. Clerics will receive new versions of these spells for each new expansion, or roughly every five levels. Also note, there are alternate advancement points that will increase the effectiveness of these spells, and they play a critical role in increasing your effectiveness at swarming.

Inventory Recommendations

This section covers the recommended inventory setup for swarming clerics. As a swarm puller you will learn to understand and value the investments of high slot bags. It's highly recommended that you purchase 4 Trademaster's Component Satchel and 3 Scuffed Journeyman's Pocketed Rucksack. It's important that your first four slots utilize tradeskill component only bags, as this will prevent any clutter from building in your normal bags and it allows you to optimally collect loot from your swarms. These bags are really worth the investment and pay for themselves literally. On top of that, they will ultimately save you time. Remember, time is money!

Get to Know the Area

Start off by making sure you have the maps for all zones and instances you have access to. It's a very simple and quick process, just head on over to Mapfiend and download a map pack. They provide easy download and installation instructions, it takes just a minute.

Look for zones that are densely populated with mobs. This enables you to optimize your experience by reducing pull times. Take advantage of the viral damage shield spell line in these zones and let it pull for you. If you can, invis your way to the back of the zone and start your pull from there. It can be a smoother experience only having to pull in one direction to your camp and not battling your way through the zone in two different directions, this is prone to more accidents happening.

Pick a Camp Location

Finding a "hard" corner to pull to is crucial. A hard corner will not have a ledge or lip that allows your character to be pushed up the wall, so be very selective. By placing your character's back to a corner, it will allow you to cast through the swarm. This will also prevent you from being knocked around and lets you control the swarm. Having the swarm placed in the corner then allows you to cast area of effect spells that will be in range of everything pulled.

It's important to make sure that mobs do not get behind your character, otherwise they can knock it out of place and begin a summoning loop. Pull the swarm in a straight line to your corner when possible.

Prepare Your Character

Preparation for the swarm pull is crucial

Before you begin each pull, there are many steps to take in order to prepare your character for a swarm pull. It’s important to make sure you take the following steps and understand why each step helps. In a static zone, make sure that the zone is empty or near empty. You will want to keep an eye on the zone populus before each pull and know what you're pulling and the areas it affects. Make sure to call out if there are others present because swarming in it's very nature is a mob train that can take out even the best of the best.

Buff Up

It’s important to know that while pulling, you do not want a damage shield on. This could cause you to be summoned and prevent you from being able to corner the swarm. Also, consider leaving room for additional buffs incase detrimental spells were to land on you. Leaving the additional space allows your important buffs to not be overwritten.

  • You have the correct buffs on.
  • Start with gear and item click effects, no damage shields yet
  • Stack spell haste, damage mitigation, and hit point buffs
  • After illusions, shrink yourself down twice
  • Stack the Vow spell line
  • Stack the aura(s), Aura of Pious and Circle of Divinity
  • Then stack the Shining Rampart & Divine Intervention spell lines before the pull
  • Additional/Extra Boost
  • Turn on Personal Tribute Points
  • Turn on Guild Tribute Points
  • Drop a Fellowship Campfire
  • Use Spider Bites Potions

The Swarm Pull

The time for onslaught has come...

Phase 1. Aggro the First Mob

How you begin the pull matters! The first mob you aggro should not be aggroed with spell or melee damage. I recommend using the viral damage shield spell line or using flash of light if you're avoiding damage all together. It's best to do this at max range, to give yourself a head start and be at the front of your swarm and not caught in the middle of it. Have your character facing the direction you intend on running, so as soon as you've aggro'd your mob, you simply press forward to start running in the direction! Using the eagle eye view is highly recommended so that you can see your swarm in it's entirety.

↑ Back to the top

Phase 2. Build Up the Swarm

Now that you've angered your first mob, the rest should be a breeze! Run in wide arcs towards clusters of mobs and make sure to keep enough distance that they aren't going to smack you, or at least smack you as little as possible. Once you've gathered your swarm, take care not to path into your swarm tail. As you move around your tail has a whip-like-motion and force to itself. While running ahead of your swarm, you can use your tail as an extension to continuing aggroing mobs from a safe distance and will no longer need to cast spells for this purpose. Be sure not to run into your tail though, as this could be potentially fatal. Begin leading your swarm in such a way that it will pick up mobs for you and keeps you out of harms way. Stay clear of raid targets as these will slow you down.

↑ Back to the top

Phase 3. Corner Yourself Up

So now you've pulled as many mobs as you can handle, make your way back to your chosen corner camp. Keep in mind any backup corners or camps you've identified when scoping the zone, in case your pull has hurt itself enough that it has begun summoning or chain stunning you, otherwise it can be very problematic. As you head back to the corner, try to pull your tail in a straight line, so that the swarm doesn't hit you from all angles.

Once you get to the corner, plant your back firmly against it, so that you will not be pushed around (this is extremely important!) and face the incoming onslaught. Right at this point you want to begin to build your defense up. Begin by the promised heal spell line on yourself, then cast the Veteran AA Armor of Experience if you have it, as it'll increases your AC substantially. Throughout the fight you will want to keep your promised heal line going. Immediately begin attacking the closest mob until your vow spell line procs a heal over time.

↑ Back to the top

Phase 4. Destroy the Swarm

Once the swarm is mostly within your range you will want to begin casting all of your damage shields, use clickies, potions, and the ward spell line. Immediately after, activate your AA Fundament: First Spire of Divinity, then the Veteran AA Intensity of the Resolute, then Battlefrenzy. This will optimize the amount of critical damage you're able to do. Once these are all activated, cast your AE stun/damage spell, then continue chain casting your AE damage spells. By the time First spire has faded, most mobs should be dead or close to it.

If your armor and alternate advancement points are not up to snuff, you may want to consider the DA Hammer + Ward spell line for killing. This method is slow, but safe. Cast a delayed/promised heal on yourself, then begin meleeing the nearest mob you can target. The proc from your hammer will place DA on you and you can use the invulnerable period to be healed by your heal over time spells. The mobs will continue to beat on you and take damage from your ward damage shield, but you will be unable to attack or cast any futher heals until your DA wears off. It's important that if you choose this method when powerleveling someone that they DO NOT ASSIST at any point, because the mob will turn on them and wipe them the instant you go invulnerable.
