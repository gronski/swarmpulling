Everquest Cleric Swarm Pulling Guide

Learn the techniques needed to master cleric swarm pulling

Clerics are one of the most powerful classes for swarm pulling in Everquest. One of the main reasons behind this, is due to the ability to cast the Divine spell line and the ability to use the Divine Hammer of Consternation which is a must-have for any cleric! This weapon allows you to proc the Divine Aura spell in the middle of combat, with no recast times or lock outs. Clerics, with their heavy armor and arsenal of utility spells, are perfectly suited to begin mass pulling at lower levels.

At level 71, clerics are able to begin using the Vow and Ward spell lines. These spells are the backbone of the cleric’s ability to swarm pull. The vow spell line will allow the cleric to proc heal over time spells on themselves while also casting a substantial direct damage spell against their target. The ward spell applies a buff to your character that has a chance to strike back against any attackers. This isn’t your normal damage shield, as it packs a punch and it also works through divine spells. Clerics will receive new versions of these spells for each new expansion, or every five levels. Also note, there are alternate advancement points that will increase the effectiveness of these spells, and they play a critical role in the upper levels.

Phase 1. Cleric Alternate Advancement Points

AA points play an important role in swarm pulling and its effectiveness is substantially increased as your character's AAs advance. Building up your character's AA points takes time, but having a specified path to work towards will help you to achieve your goals. Additionally, it will aid you in understanding your character's growth and keep you wanting more.

There are four primary sections that have been broken down below to help you focus on the most important areas of your character's AA development. The order of importance is from top to bottom for each section. You will want to have a balanced approach as you gain higher levels and AAs are more costly to purchase. Phase 2. Selecting a Perfect Zone and Camp Spot

Selecting the location to center your efforts can be challenging. Certain zones will prove to be more appealing depending on what you’re aiming for. There are a few things to consider and doing your homework will save you from wasting your time.

  • Are you looking for experience?
  • Power Leveling
  • Grinding Regular Experience
  • Grinding Alternate Advancement Points
  • What are you farming?
  • Armor/Gear
  • Platinum
  • Quests/Missions
  • Tradeskill Items
  • Zone specifics?
  • Level Range
  • Mob Density
  • Summoning Capabilities
  • Melee or Caster Types

Look for zones that are densely populated with mobs. This enables you to optimize your experience by reducing pull times. Take advantage of the viral damage shield spell line in these zones and let it pull for you. If you can, invis your way to the back of the zone and start your pull from there. It's a smoother experience only having to pull to your camp and not battling your way through the zone in two different directions.

Finding a "hard" corner to pull to is crucial. A hard corner will not have a ledge or lip that allows your character to be pushed up the wall, so be very selective. By placing your character's back to a corner, it will allow you to cast through the swarm. This will also prevent you from being knocked around and lets you control the swarm. Having the swarm placed in the corner then allows you to cast area of effect spells that will be in range of everything pulled.

It's important to make sure that mobs do not get behind your character, otherwise they can knock it out of place and begin a summoning loop. Pull the swarm in a straight line when possible to aid in this.
